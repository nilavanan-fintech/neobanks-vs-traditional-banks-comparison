Neobanks vs Traditional Banks

A financial performance & customer experience comparison — benchmarking a digital-first neobank (Jupiter, Fi Money) against an incumbent bank on cost efficiency, customer acquisition cost, NPS, and product depth.

Traditional Bank — branch ledger economics vs Neobank — app-first unit economics
01 · Framing

What the project proves

A tight, defensible thesis is what separates a portfolio piece from a data dump. Anchor the whole project on one question.

Traditional Bank

  • Owns a full banking licence — deposits, lending, and balance sheet are native.
  • Cost structure carries branches, staff, and legacy core-banking systems.
  • Acquires customers through trust, physical presence, and cross-sell of existing relationships.
  • Example anchors: HDFC Bank, ICICI Bank, SBI, Kotak Mahindra Bank.

Neobank

  • No standalone banking licence in India — operates as a tech layer on a partner bank (e.g. Jupiter × Federal Bank, Fi Money × Federal Bank).
  • Lean cost base, but customer acquisition is paid, design-led, and expensive to sustain.
  • Monetises via interchange, lending referrals, and premium subscriptions rather than net interest margin alone.
  • Example anchors: Jupiter, Fi Money, Niyo, Open.

Suggested thesis: "Neobanks win on experience and product velocity but trail traditional banks on cost-to-income and profitability — evaluate whether the CX premium justifies the unit-economics gap." Pick one neobank and one traditional bank so every metric has a clean head-to-head.

04 · Data Sources

Where each metric actually comes from

Neither institution publishes a clean "NPS vs CAC vs cost efficiency" table — you are assembling this from disclosed financials, secondary research, and your own primary survey.

PillarPrimary SourceWhat you'll extract
Cost efficiency MCA/RoC filings via Tofler or Zauba Corp; bank annual reports (investor relations pages) Revenue, net loss/profit, total opex — to compute cost-to-income ratio and burn multiple, e.g. Jupiter's FY24 net loss of ₹275.9 Cr against ₹35.8 Cr revenue.
Customer acquisition cost Funding disclosures (Inc42, Entrackr, Tracxn), marketing spend line-items in filings Total sales & marketing spend ÷ new customers added in the period, for both entities where disclosed.
NPS (proxy) Google Play Store & Apple App Store reviews; your own primary survey Star ratings + review sentiment scored into promoter/detractor buckets; validate with a real 50–100 response survey.
Product offerings Bank/neobank websites, app feature lists, RBI's list of licensed banks A binary/scored feature matrix — savings, FD, cards, UPI, lending, investing, insurance, forex, etc.
Industry benchmarks RBI annual reports & trend/progress of banking in India report, Statista, sector reports (e.g. UV Netware, Inc42, Inventiva neobank coverage) Sector-level context — market size, funding trends, profitability rates across the neobank category, to frame your two-entity comparison.

Reality check to state in your report: most Indian neobanks don't disclose an official NPS, and several — including Jupiter and Fi Money — don't publish full audited financials the way listed banks do. Treat neobank figures as best-available secondary data and be explicit about which numbers are self-reported, modelled, or proxy-derived.

05 · Metrics Framework

The formulas behind each pillar

Cost-to-Income Ratio

Operating Cost ÷ Operating Income × 100

Lower is better. Traditional banks typically land 40–55%; loss-making neobanks often exceed 100% because opex outpaces revenue.

Burn Multiple

Net Cash Burned ÷ Net New Revenue

A neobank-specific efficiency lens — e.g. spending close to ₹10 to earn ₹1 of revenue signals a burn multiple near 10x.

Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)

Total Acquisition Spend ÷ New Customers Added

Compare against branch-led acquisition cost for the traditional bank, which is typically lower per-customer but slower to scale.

Proxy Net Promoter Score

%Promoters (4–5★, positive sentiment) − %Detractors (1–2★, negative sentiment)

Run this on ≥300 recent reviews per app for statistical stability, then cross-check against your own survey's real NPS question.

Product Depth Score

Σ (feature present × feature weight) ÷ max possible score

Weight features by relevance (e.g. lending and investing weighted higher than cosmetic UI features).

Composite Comparison Index

Weighted average of the 4 normalised pillar scores (0–100 each)

Feeds directly into the radar chart — this is the single number your executive summary can lead with.
